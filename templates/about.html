About Us

With Tumor

Atypical teratoid/rhabdoid tumor (AT/RT) is a rare, aggressive brain tumor most commonly affecting young children. It originates from the central nervous system and is characterized by fast growth and resistance to treatment. AT/RT often presents with symptoms like headaches, nausea, and neurological deficits due to increased intracranial pressure. Treatment typically involves a combination of surgery, chemotherapy, and radiation, but prognosis remains poor, with survival rates significantly lower than other pediatric brain tumors.

Without Tumor

The brain, a complex organ, controls thoughts, emotions, and bodily functions. Without an atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumor (ATRT), a rare, aggressive cancer primarily in children, the brain can function optimally. ATRT originates in the brain or spinal cord, causing neurological symptoms like headaches, nausea, and motor skill impairments. Absence of ATRT means normal brain development, cognitive abilities, and reduced neurological health risks. Regular check-ups and healthy lifestyle choices support brain health.

How Tumor Forms

Atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumors (ATRT) are rare, aggressive brain and spinal cord tumors primarily affecting children aged between 3-6 years. They originate from cells with genetic mutations, notably in the SMARCB1 (INI1) gene, which is crucial for cell regulation and tumor suppression. When this gene mutates or is deleted, cells grow uncontrollably, forming tumors. ATRTs can develop in various brain regions and are characterized by rapid growth and a high potential for metastasis, making early detection and treatment critical.

The signs and symptoms of AT/RT

  • morning headache or headache that goes away after vomiting.
  • nausea and vomiting.
  • unusual sleepiness or change in activity level.
  • loss of balance, lack of coordination, or trouble walking.
  • increase in head size (in infants)

Precautions for ATRT Tumor

  • Regular medical follow-ups: Monitor tumor growth and health status.
  • Consult with specialists: Neurologist, oncologist, for comprehensive treatment plans.
  • Adhere to treatment protocols: Surgery, chemotherapy, radiation as prescribed.
  • Maintain healthy lifestyle: Nutrition, exercise, sleep to support recovery.
  • Monitor for symptoms: Seizures, headaches, changes in behavior or coordination.
