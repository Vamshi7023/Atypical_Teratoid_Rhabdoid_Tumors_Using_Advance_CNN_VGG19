Proudly Presenting AT/RTs

An atypical teratoid rhabdoid tumor (ATRT) is a rare, aggressive central nervous system tumor, often found in the cerebellum or brain stem. Mostly occurring by age 3, ATRTs can also affect older children. They represent 1-2% of childhood brain tumors and belong to a larger group of malignant rhabdoid tumors, which can occur outside the brain. ATRTs may be localized or spread to other parts of the brain, spine, or body.
